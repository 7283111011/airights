Project 03

Public Education and AI Literacy

Public understanding of artificial intelligence is critically low, and teachers consistently report that they have neither training nor policy guidance. FAIR is developing an evidence based programme of public engagement, professional briefings, and parliamentary commentary.

Project Lead
To be appointed on formation of the Board
Status
In planning
Workstream
Public engagement

Background

The 2026 NEU Survey reported that only nine per cent of UK teachers feel confident teaching about AI, while seventy six per cent are using AI in their daily work. The Pearson School Report of December 2025 found that forty nine per cent of schools have no AI policy at all. Two thirds of secondary teachers report measurable declines in critical thinking among pupils. Parliament has acknowledged the gap, but no comprehensive curriculum or workforce development response has been delivered.

What FAIR proposes

  • An accessible curriculum of plain language briefings on every significant AI policy development before Parliament.
  • A practitioner facing professional series for solicitors, barristers, doctors, accountants, and educators on the implications of AI for their practice.
  • A schools and further education programme delivered in partnership with established educational charities and learned societies.
  • A standing public consultation channel through which the public can raise specific concerns about AI deployment.
  • An annual State of AI Literacy report.

Relevant legislation and instruments

  • UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR)
  • Data Protection Act 2018
  • Equality Act 2010
  • Education Act 2002
