Project 04

AI Transparency and Accountability

The UK currently imposes no binding transparency obligation upon AI developers. FAIR will advocate for statutory disclosure obligations covering training data, system instructions, and any commercial arrangements that influence AI outputs.

Project Lead
To be appointed on formation of the Board
Status
In planning
Workstream
Technical standards

Background

Research presented at FAccT 2025 and CHI 2026 documented that hidden system prompts in widely used AI products introduce systematic bias without users’ knowledge, and that most providers actively instruct their models to conceal these instructions from the public. There is no statutory requirement in the UK for AI developers to disclose the data on which their models are trained, the system level instructions they receive, or the commercial arrangements that influence their outputs.

What FAIR proposes

  • A statutory transparency obligation requiring all general purpose AI developers offering services in the UK to publish a structured summary of training data sources.
  • A duty to disclose, on request, the system level instructions applied to a model in a given product context.
  • A duty to disclose any commercial agreement or sponsorship that materially influences model outputs.
  • An independent technical audit framework, modelled on financial audit, with cooperation between regulator and developer.
  • A right of redress for individuals adversely affected by undisclosed system instructions or training data choices.

Relevant legislation and instruments

  • EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689), article 53 (transparency obligations for general purpose AI)
  • ICO AI Code of Practice (May 2026)
  • UK GDPR, articles 13, 14, and 22
  • Data Protection Act 2018
  • Equality Act 2010
