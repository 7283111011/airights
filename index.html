Three objectives.

The Foundation works on three fronts: showing the public where artificial intelligence is used, pressing for stronger law, and helping people understand what it means for them.

Awareness

We track where artificial intelligence is being used across United Kingdom public services and publish it openly, so the public can see what is happening.

Open AI Alerts →

Legislation

We make independent, evidence-led submissions to Parliament, regulators and Government, pressing for law that protects the public.

Read our work →

Learning

We help people of every age and background understand artificial intelligence and the rights it engages, written in plain English.

Built for public accountability.

The Foundation for Artificial Intelligence Rights exists to increase public understanding of artificial intelligence and the rights it engages. We show how it is used across public services, press for stronger protections in law, and hold the bodies that deploy it to account.

Independent. Evidence-led. United Kingdom focused.

“Most of my career, I have supported drug development by helping organisations navigate complex regulatory requirements, ensuring patient safety and rights are upheld whilst generating data of the highest integrity.” Scott McCulloch · Founder, FAIR
