FAIR is a UK charity advancing public understanding of artificial intelligence and the rights it engages, and supporting better-informed decisions on how AI is developed, deployed, and governed. We provide research, briefings, and evidence to Parliament, the regulators, and the wider professional community on the legal, ethical, and societal questions AI now raises.

Register Interest Our Mission

AI systems are being deployed at scale across healthcare, education, financial services, and the wider public sector, yet the UK has, to date, declined to introduce comprehensive statutory safeguards.

No domestic legislation imposes binding transparency obligations upon AI developers. There exists no independent audit framework, no statutory duty of disclosure as to training data, system instructions, or commercial arrangements influencing model outputs, and no proper redress for individuals subject to opaque automated decisions. The AI (Regulation) Bill, having progressed through earlier parliamentary stages, remains stalled. Successive Governments have deferred substantive action.

FAIR was founded to advance, through evidence-based research and principled engagement with Parliament, the enforceable rights and meaningful protections to which the public are properly entitled.

Built on the principle that innovation and accountability must go together

In industries like pharmaceuticals, aviation, and financial services, innovation operates within rigorous oversight frameworks because the consequences of failure are too serious for voluntary commitments. AI carries comparable risks to individuals and to society, yet it operates in a regulatory vacuum. FAIR exists to inform Parliament, the regulators, and the public about how rights, transparency, and accountability can be upheld in the context of AI; and to support evidence-based decisions in this important area of public policy.

01

Rights and Public Education

Advancing public understanding of the rights engaged by AI — from the protection of creative works and personal data to safeguards against opaque automated decisions — and the legal frameworks in which those rights operate.

02

Policy & Regulatory Engagement

Providing research, technical briefings, and written evidence to Select Committees, regulators, and Parliamentarians, so that decisions on AI policy and legislation are informed by independent, evidence-based analysis.

03

Education and Public Accountability

Developing evidence based AI literacy programmes, publishing plain language policy briefings, and monitoring AI developments across Westminster, Whitehall, and the regulatory bodies.
