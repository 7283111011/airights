Project 05

Policy and Regulatory Engagement

FAIR will engage directly with Parliamentarians, submit expert evidence to Select Committees, draft model provisions, and work with cross party groups to ensure the forthcoming AI Bill contains enforceable rights and genuine accountability.

Project Lead
To be appointed on formation of the Board
Status
In planning
Workstream
Core function

Background

The AI (Regulation) Bill, having progressed through earlier parliamentary stages, has stalled. Successive Governments have deferred comprehensive AI legislation. The 2025 King’s Speech and the 2026 Action Plan signalled a continued preference for non statutory mechanisms. FAIR considers that this position is no longer tenable in light of documented harms across healthcare, education, financial services, child safety, and the creative industries.

What FAIR will deliver

  • Expert written and oral evidence to relevant Select Committees, including the Science, Innovation and Technology Committee and the House of Lords AI in Weapon Systems Committee, where invited.
  • Draft model provisions corresponding to each of FAIR’s priority areas, suitable for incorporation into a Government bill or a Private Member’s bill.
  • Direct engagement with Parliamentarians on a cross party basis.
  • Public commentary on every significant AI policy development through plain language briefings.
  • Submissions to Government and regulatory consultations on AI matters.

Relevant legislation and instruments

  • AI (Regulation) Bill
  • AI Opportunities Action Plan 2026
  • King’s Speech 2026 legislative programme
  • House of Commons Standing Orders relating to Select Committee evidence
