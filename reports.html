Reports & Publications

FAIR’s annual reports, accounts, founding documents, and publications, published openly in line with our commitment to good governance.

Last updated: 1 May 2026 · Version 1.0

FAIR is committed to public transparency. Once registered as a Charitable Incorporated Organisation, FAIR will publish its annual report, statement of accounts, and Trustees’ report each financial year, in line with the Charities (Accounts and Reports) Regulations 2008 and the Charities SORP.

Annual Report and Accounts

FAIR’s first Annual Report and Accounts will cover the period from registration to the end of the first full financial year. The report will include:

  • The Trustees’ report: activities, achievements, and performance against the theory of change.
  • A statement of financial activities and balance sheet, prepared in accordance with the Charities SORP (FRS 102).
  • Trustees’ declarations and a register of relevant interests, in line with our Conflicts of Interest Policy.
  • A safeguarding and serious-incident summary.
  • A complaints summary.

Until the first Annual Report is published, the documents set out below give an indication of FAIR’s purpose, programme, and governance.

Founding documents

  • Constitution: CIO model (to be published on registration).
  • Theory of change
  • Privacy Policy
  • Safeguarding Policy
  • Conflicts of Interest Policy
  • Complaints Procedure
  • Modern Slavery Statement (voluntary)

Publications and submissions

Working papers, policy briefings, and submissions to Parliament and regulators will be archived here as FAIR publishes them. To be notified, write to info@airights.org.uk.

Charity Commission record

Once registered, FAIR’s public record, including its registered objects, current trustees, and filed accounts, will be available on the Charity Commission’s public register.
