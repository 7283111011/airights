Project 01

AI Copyright and Intellectual Property Reform

Reforming the United Kingdom’s copyright framework to provide a balanced commercial text and data mining exception, mandatory training data disclosure, producer rights reform, and a market access provision for models trained outside the United Kingdom.

Project Lead
To be appointed on formation of the Board
Status
In planning
Workstream
Legislative reform

Background

Section 29A of the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 permits text and data mining only for non commercial research. There is no equivalent commercial safe harbour. The position of UK rights holders is further weakened by the absence of any meaningful transparency obligation on AI developers, and by the absence of effective enforcement mechanisms where infringement is suspected. The Government’s December 2024 consultation on AI and copyright closed without delivering a settled policy.

What FAIR proposes

  • A balanced commercial text and data mining exception, conditional on mandatory disclosure of training data sources.
  • A statutory transparency requirement applying to all foundation model developers placing services on the United Kingdom market.
  • A producer rights reform package extending and clarifying the protection afforded to creators whose work is ingested for training.
  • A market access provision requiring extra territorial compliance for models trained abroad and offered in the United Kingdom.
  • A streamlined enforcement mechanism allowing rights holders to obtain disclosure and meaningful remedy.

Relevant legislation and instruments

  • Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988, sections 29A and 16
  • EU Directive on Copyright in the Digital Single Market 2019/790, articles 3 and 4
  • EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689), article 53
  • Data (Use and Access) Act 2025
  • UK Government response to AI and copyright consultation, 2025
