AI and Child Safety

Statutory protections against the most serious harms generative AI now poses to children, including nudification tools, deepfake generators, and AI companion chatbots. The current enforcement framework remains underdeveloped despite documented harm.

Project Lead
To be appointed on formation of the Board
Status
In planning
Workstream
Safeguarding

Background

Ofcom fined the operator of an AI nudification site in November 2025 and opened formal investigations into AI chatbot services in January 2026. The proposed Keeping Children Safe in Education 2026 guidance formally recognises AI generated abuse material as a safeguarding concern. The Crime and Policing Bill proposes prohibitions on certain tools, but its scope is narrow and its enforcement provisions remain underdeveloped. Existing duties under the Online Safety Act 2023 do not adequately address AI specific risks.

What FAIR proposes

  • Statutory age gating, age assurance, and content provenance standards applicable to providers of generative AI services accessible from the UK.
  • Criminal liability for the operators of platforms that fail to implement reasonable safeguards against the production of AI generated child sexual abuse material.
  • Expanded reporting and takedown obligations integrated with the Internet Watch Foundation and the National Crime Agency.
  • Mandatory cooperation between AI developers, hosting providers, and law enforcement on the rapid removal of harmful content.
  • A statutory code of practice for AI companion services aimed at minors.

Relevant legislation and instruments

  • Online Safety Act 2023
  • Crime and Policing Bill (currently before Parliament)
  • Keeping Children Safe in Education 2026 (DfE consultation)
  • Protection of Children Act 1978
  • Sexual Offences Act 2003
