FAIR Briefings

Last updated: 1 May 2026 · Version 1.0
FAIR Briefings. A short, plain-English digest of UK AI policy, sent every two weeks. Parliamentary inquiries worth knowing about, regulator consultations worth responding to, court judgments worth reading, and the occasional FAIR briefing on a specific question. No tracking, no upsell, easy to unsubscribe at any point.

What you can expect

  • Cadence: fortnightly, usually on a Friday afternoon.
  • Length: short. Roughly 700 to 1,000 words, takes three to five minutes to read.
  • Format: plain-text email. No HTML images, no tracking pixels.
  • Topics: open UK AI policy consultations and their deadlines; recent court judgments on AI, copyright, and data protection; published regulator decisions; brief notes on FAIR’s own work; selected reading.
  • Cost: free.
  • Unsubscribe: one click, on any issue. The link is at the bottom of every email.

Sign up

Enter your email address below. You will receive a confirmation email at that address; clicking the link in that email completes the sign-up.

By subscribing you confirm you are happy to receive the FAIR Briefings newsletter at the address provided. Subscription data is processed by Buttondown Inc on FAIR’s behalf and on the lawful basis of consent. See our Privacy Policy for details. You can withdraw consent at any time by clicking the unsubscribe link in any issue.

What FAIR does with subscriber data

  • We keep your email address only for the purpose of sending the newsletter. We do not share or sell it.
  • We process subscription data using Buttondown (Buttondown Inc, US-based, with appropriate UK GDPR transfer safeguards), as our data processor.
  • We do not place tracking pixels in our newsletter, and we do not record whether you opened or clicked anything in any issue.
  • Aggregate, anonymous open and click counts may be reported by the email service in summary form; FAIR does not retain or use this data.
  • You can unsubscribe at any time using the link at the bottom of every issue, or by emailing info@airights.org.uk with the word UNSUBSCRIBE in the subject line.
